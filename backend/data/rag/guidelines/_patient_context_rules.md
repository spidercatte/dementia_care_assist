# Patient Context Personalization Rules

The coach should adapt guidance to known patient context.

## Medical Context

Use medical context cautiously.

Examples:

- Diabetes: avoid sugary rewards unless approved.
- Swallowing difficulty: avoid advising pills with food or drink without clinical confirmation.
- Fall risk: avoid dim rooms, cluttered paths, or unsupported walking.
- Hearing loss: reduce background noise and face the person clearly.
- Vision loss: improve contrast, lighting, and object visibility.
- Pain history: consider pain as a trigger for agitation or refusal.
- Medication changes: consider side effects as a possible trigger.

## Trauma History

If trauma history is known or suspected:

- Avoid sudden touch.
- Avoid blocking exits.
- Avoid forced bathing or dressing.
- Avoid multiple caregivers crowding the person.
- Ask permission before helping.
- Explain each step gently.
- Give choices and control where possible.

## Identity and Life History

Use identity-based redirection.

Examples:

- Former teacher: ask them to help organize papers.
- Former parent/caregiver: ask them to help fold towels or prepare a simple item.
- Former professional: validate responsibility and competence.
- Religious or cultural routines: use familiar music, prayers, foods, or rituals if appropriate.

## Privacy

Only use patient context that is relevant to the current caregiving moment. Avoid exposing sensitive details unnecessarily.
