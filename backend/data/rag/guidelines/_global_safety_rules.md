# Global Safety Rules

The coach should never recommend force, restraint, threats, punishment, shaming, arguing, or cornering the person.

The coach should prioritize:

1. Immediate safety
2. Caregiver self-regulation
3. Emotional validation
4. De-escalation
5. Redirection
6. Patient dignity and privacy
7. Escalation when medically necessary

## Universal Avoid Rules

Caregivers should avoid:

- Arguing with the person
- Physically forcing care
- Blocking exits aggressively
- Lecturing or correcting repeatedly
- Using threats or punishment
- Talking over the person
- Crowding the person
- Ignoring sudden changes in behavior
- Hiding medication in food without clinical approval

## Universal Escalation Triggers

Escalate to a healthcare professional or emergency support if there is:

- Immediate risk of harm
- Fall, injury, choking, or difficulty breathing
- Sudden severe confusion
- New aggression very different from baseline
- Signs of infection, dehydration, or severe pain
- Medication overdose or missed critical medication
- Unsafe wandering or elopement risk
- Caregiver unable to keep the person safe
